NEW MEXICO HISTORY

11000 to 9200BCE

Earliest archaeological evidence of human civilisation in New Mexico.

300BCE to CE1600

Settlement of Pueblo people developed throughout New Mexico. Many advancements made in farming, irrigation, gardening skills, pottery making, weapons for hunting and refined housing technique to adobe buildings that we still see today. Culture flourished in the rich and fertile Rio Grande Valley. Today, there are 23 Native American tribes of which 19 are Pueblo, 3 Apache and Navajo Nation.

16th Century

Spanish exploration and colonisation of New Mexico. Initially seeking gold, they returned 50 years later with 500 Spanish settlers and soldiers and the first Spanish settlement in New Mexico on 11 July 1598 as part of “New Spain”. The province was called Santa Fe de Nuevo México. During this settlement. During this occupation, the Natives revolted but many indigenous lives were lost.

1610

The settlement of Santa Fe was established at the foot of the Sangre de Cristo mountains. Santa Fe is the oldest state capital in the States and the highest elevation at 7000ft (2100m) above sea level.

1680

Pueblo Revolt. After many years are hostility towards Spanish oppression of indigenous in New Mexico, and the introduction of European diseases which killed off many natives, a revolt was orchestrated by Popé (or Po-pay), a religious leader from one of the New Mexico pueblos. They successfully drove the Spaniards from all but Southern, New Mexico. This left the Natives in control of New Mexico. Unfortunately, in 1692, Spanish forces once again took the capital, offering clemency to Natives who swore allegiance to the King of Spain.

1706

Albuquerque was founded (La Villa de Alburquerque) after the Spanish Duke of Alburquerque. This was on the El Camino Real, linking Mexico City to the Northern territory of New Spain. Today, Albuquerque is the most populous city in New Mexico with a population as of 2020 of 916,528 inclusive of the metro area.

19th Century

Mexican independence from the Spanish is won and New Mexico become of a territory of United Mexican States. During this time, trade along the Santa Fe trail was opened which opened trade with the United States and Mexico. 1846 to 1848 saw the Mexican-American War when the United States Army invaded Mexico and resulted in the Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo in 1848 where the United States was granted most of the northern Mexico territories. This is the American Southwest and California we know today.

6 January 1912

New Mexico becomes the 47th state of the United States of America.

16 July 1945

The world’s first atomic bomb was tested at the Trinity Site in Southern New Mexico.

Today

New Mexico is the 5th largest State by area but is one of the least populated, ranking number 36th. It is a very diverse State which has the highest population of Hispanic and Latino Americans and the 2nd highest percentage of Native Americans. The landscape is as diverse as its people, with forested mountains to deserts. You can enjoy skiing and winter sports in the north and east and much warmer and arid climates in the south and west. There are 3 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, which is more than any other state. It is truly the Land of Enchantment.
