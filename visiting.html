Albuquerque


Albuquerque is New Mexico’s largest city and, like most cities, has a vibrant nightlife but is also packed with historic places and fun things to do, whether travelling solo or with family and/or friends. If you get tired of the city life, travelling less than an hour will get you back to nature in the Sandia Mountains to the east or the Rio Grande bosque which runs through the middle of the city.


THINGS TO DO

Sandia Peak Tramway, Old Town, Indian Pueblo Cultural Center, ABQ Bio Park Botanic Garden.

Santa Fe


Santa Fe is the capital of New Mexico and the oldest state capital. Sitting at over 7000ft (2100m) above sea-level, it is also the highest. It is a melting pot of culture and history aptly nicknamed ‘The City Different’. Considered one of the greatest art cities in the USA, Santa Fe is an art lover’s paradise. Visiting one of its many markets or enjoying some of the best restaurants in New Mexico is something every traveller should do.


THINGS TO DO

Santa Fe Plaza, Georgia O’Keefe museum, Meow Wolf, Canyon Road, Loretto Chapel, Museum of International Folk Art

MORE INFO

Visit Santa Fe website


Taos


If you are looking for the bustle and excitement of a big city, Taos is not the place to be. Taos is a place of tranquillity, art, nature and culture. Located in the Sangre de Cristo mountains, Taos has an energy unlike any other place. You can get a drink and listen to live music in the town’s plaza in the summer or hit the slopes at Taos Ski Valley in the winter. If that doesn’t interest you, then rafting in the Rio Grande may be the way to go.


THINGS TO DO

Taos Pueblo (UNESCO World Heritage site), Rio Grande gorge bridge, rafting on the Rio Grande, historic Taos Plaza, Taos earthships

MORE INFO

Visit Taos website


Las Cruces


Situated in southern New Mexico, Las Cruces is closer to Mexico than to Albuquerque. This is the second largest of New Mexico’s cities and is enjoyed by visitors and residents for its temperate climate year-round and over 350 days of clear, blue skies. With the Organ mountains to the east, visitors can enjoy a day of hiking before heading into the city for some good food and New Mexican wine.


THINGS TO DO

Dripping Springs Natural Area, Old Mesilla Village, New Mexico Farm and Ranch Heritage Museum, Mesilla Plaza

MORE INFO

Visit Las Cruces website


Roswell


Roswell is primarily known for the UFO incident in 1947 where some believe the crash was an alien spaceship which is being covered up by the military. Others, however, believe that the crash was just debris from a military balloon. Whatever your belief, Roswell offers much more than aliens and spaceships. Located in the High Great Plains of southeastern New Mexico, there are many beautiful state parks nearby to ponder such historic mysteries.


THINGS TO DO

Roswell Museum, International UFO Museum and Research Center, Bottomless Lakes State Park, Bitter Lake National Wildlife Refuge

MORE INFO

Visit Roswell website


Silver City


You may have guessed from its name, Silver City was once a mining boom town in the 1800s. Battles with the nearby Apache Indians and with troublemakers like Billy the Kid made Silver City a lawless place and truly the wild west. Today, Silver City is a sleepy town with a population under 10,000. Located in southwestern New Mexico, it hosts many music and art festivals. A short journey away from Silver City you will find the Gila Cliff Dwellings National Monument and several lakes.


THINGS TO DO

Gila Cliff Dwellings National Monument, Silver City Visitor Center, Silver City Museum, Lake Roberts

MORE INFO

Visit Silver City website
