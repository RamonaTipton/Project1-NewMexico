VISITING

New Mexico Cities


While cities in New Mexico aren't on the scale of other states, what they lack in cosmopolitan flare they make up for in a diverse offering of cuisine, nightlife and daytime activities both in and around. Here are some top destinations.


Albuquerque


Albuquerque is New Mexico’s largest city and, like most cities, has a vibrant nightlife but is also packed with historic places and fun things to do, whether travelling solo or with family and/or friends. If you get tired of the city life, travelling less than an hour will get you back to nature in the Sandia Mountains to the east or the Rio Grande bosque which runs through the middle of the city.


THINGS TO DO

Sandia Peak Tramway, Old Town, Indian Pueblo Cultural Center, ABQ Bio Park Botanic Garden.

MORE INFO

Visit Albuquerque website


Santa Fe


Santa Fe is the capital of New Mexico and the oldest state capital. Sitting at over 7000ft (2100m) above sea-level, it is also the highest. It is a melting pot of culture and history aptly nicknamed ‘The City Different’. Considered one of the greatest art cities in the USA, Santa Fe is an art lover’s paradise. Visiting one of its many markets or enjoying some of the best restaurants in New Mexico is something every traveller should do.


THINGS TO DO

Santa Fe Plaza, Georgia O’Keefe museum, Meow Wolf, Canyon Road, Loretto Chapel, Museum of International Folk Art

MORE INFO

Visit Santa Fe website


Taos


If you are looking for the bustle and excitement of a big city, Taos is not the place to be. Taos is a place of tranquillity, art, nature and culture. Located in the Sangre de Cristo mountains, Taos has an energy unlike any other place. You can get a drink and listen to live music in the town’s plaza in the summer or hit the slopes at Taos Ski Valley in the winter. If that doesn’t interest you, then rafting in the Rio Grande may be the way to go.


THINGS TO DO

Taos Pueblo (UNESCO World Heritage site), Rio Grande gorge bridge, rafting on the Rio Grande, historic Taos Plaza, Taos earthships

MORE INFO

Visit Taos website


Las Cruces


Situated in southern New Mexico, Las Cruces is closer to Mexico than to Albuquerque. This is the second largest of New Mexico’s cities and is enjoyed by visitors and residents for its temperate climate year-round and over 350 days of clear, blue skies. With the Organ mountains to the east, visitors can enjoy a day of hiking before heading into the city for some good food and New Mexican wine.


THINGS TO DO

Dripping Springs Natural Area, Old Mesilla Village, New Mexico Farm and Ranch Heritage Museum, Mesilla Plaza

MORE INFO

Visit Las Cruces website


Roswell


Roswell is primarily known for the UFO incident in 1947 where some believe the crash was an alien spaceship which is being covered up by the military. Others, however, believe that the crash was just debris from a military balloon. Whatever your belief, Roswell offers much more than aliens and spaceships. Located in the High Great Plains of southeastern New Mexico, there are many beautiful state parks nearby to ponder such historic mysteries.


THINGS TO DO

Roswell Museum, International UFO Museum and Research Center, Bottomless Lakes State Park, Bitter Lake National Wildlife Refuge

MORE INFO

Visit Roswell website


Silver City


You may have guessed from its name, Silver City was once a mining boom town in the 1800s. Battles with the nearby Apache Indians and with troublemakers like Billy the Kid made Silver City a lawless place and truly the wild west. Today, Silver City is a sleepy town with a population under 10,000. Located in southwestern New Mexico, it hosts many music and art festivals. A short journey away from Silver City you will find the Gila Cliff Dwellings National Monument and several lakes.


THINGS TO DO

Gila Cliff Dwellings National Monument, Silver City Visitor Center, Silver City Museum, Lake Roberts

MORE INFO

Visit Silver City website

Notable Sites


There are so many beautiful and historic places to visit in New Mexico. Here are some of the top destinations that tourists love.


Carlsbad Caverns National Park


Discovered in 1898 by a teenager, Carlsbad Caverns attracts thousands of visitors each year. Located in the Chihuahuan Desert of southern New Mexico, it has over 100 caves featuring large calcite deposits called stalactites and stalagmites which formed over hundreds of thousands of years. The largest is a chamber called the Big Room which is almost 4000ft (1220m) long and 625ft (191m) wide which makes it one of the largest in the world. It was granted UNESCO World Heritage status in 1995.


MORE INFO

Visit Carlsbad Caverns website

White Sands


The stunning pure white gypsum dune fields of White Sands National Park located in southern New Mexico leaves visitors awestruck. The largest of its kind on earth, the gypsum dune field covers 275 square miles. It is the most visited National Park in New Mexico with over 600,000 visitors each year who can hike, sled down the dunes, or just take in the beauty of the pure white sand against the clear, blue sky.


MORE INFO

Visit White Sands website

Gila Cliff Dwellings


The archaeological preservation and wonder of Gila Cliff Dwellings National Monument give tourists a glimpse into New Mexico history. Although evidence suggests that Pueblo people inhabited the caves from 900 to 1150AD, the dwellings were built by the Mogollon people between 1270 and 1300. The dwellings were built in five caves with up to 40 rooms per dwelling housing 10 to 15 families. Today, due to the preservation proclamation in 1907, 40% of the walls retain the original mortar.


MORE INFO

Visit Gila Cliff Dwellings website

Chaco Culture National Historical Park


Located in northwestern New Mexico, Chaco Canyon was given UNESCO World Heritage status in 1987 due to it being a major centre of Pueblo culture for over 2000 years and are today considered sacred by Hopi and Pueblo people. The scale of the ruins suggests an advanced engineering ability not seen anywhere else. It is said that for thousands of Ancestral Puebloans Chaco Canyon was used for ceremonial and pollical activities and trade between 850 and 1250AD.


MORE INFO

Visit Chaco website

Lincoln Historic Site


Stories of the wild west permeated the world and are the basis for Western movies and some of the most famous Hollywood movie stars like John Wayne and Clint Eastwood. Lincoln, New Mexico is one of the places you can still see what the west was like when it was wild with original buildings preserved and turned into museums. Here you will find the Lincoln County Courthouse where Billy the Kid escaped from the jail, killing Deputy James Bell. The preserved bullet hole can be seen today.


MORE INFO

Visit Lincoln website

Petroglyph National Monument


To the west of Albuquerque is Petroglyph National Monument which has over 24,000 petroglyph carvings by Ancestral Puebloans. Some date as far back as 3000 years ago, although 90% are estimated to have been created between 1300 and 1700AD. It is one of the largest petroglyph sites in North America. The monument lies on a chain of dormant volcanoes and there are several trails to allow visitors to see the petroglyphs and nature that surrounds them.

Exciting Events


Some of the events that take place across New Mexico are world-renowned. Visitors come from all over to partake in these events, celebrating the culture and diversity of the state.



Albuquerque International Balloon Fiesta


It is said that the Albuquerque Balloon Fiesta is the world’s most photographed event which in 2022 saw over 800,000 visitors. Each October, hundreds of hot air balloons fill the skies of Albuquerque enchanting spectators from all over the world. Beginning early each morning for the dawn raid, visitors can mingle among the pilots readying balloons for take-off. Other events include the balloon glow and special shapes rodeo.


WHEN

Annually, early October

MORE INFO

Visit Balloon Fiesta website


Burning of Zozobra


Zozobra or “Old Man Gloom” is a 50-foot-high effigy representing gloom and anxiety. During the year, people can write down their troubles or add documents that cause them strife. These are then added to Zozobra’s stuffing and on the nominated night, spectators gather to watch Zozobra burn along with their troubles and worries. 2023 will see the 99th burning of Zozobra and will kick off the annual Fiestas de Santa Fe.


WHEN

Annually, early September.

MORE INFO

Visit Zozobra website


New Mexico Wine Festival


When people think of wine, they probably don’t think of New Mexico. The first wine grapes were planted in 1629 by a Franciscan friar in the middle Rio Grande valley. Today there are more than 60 wineries which produce over 3.4 million litres of wine a year. The New Mexico Wine Festival celebrates New Mexican wine each spring over 3 days celebrating wine with music, art and other activities.


WHEN

Annually, Memorial Day weekend

MORE INFO

Visit New Mexico wine website


Gathering of Nations Pow Wow


Each year over 700 tribes across the US and Canada come together in Albuquerque for the largest pow-wow in North America. Along with the Miss Indian World pageant, indigenous people can don the ceremonial dress of their ancestors, competing in dance, singing and dance competitions. Not only an amazing spectacle, but it is also deemed a significant cultural event.


WHEN

Annually, 4th weekend in April

MORE INFO

Visit Gathering of Nations website


Hatch Chile Festival


Red, green or Christmas is the most asked question in New Mexico for the preference of the state’s prized Hatch chiles. They come in many forms making up New Mexican cuisine. Hatch Valley in the south is where much of the chile crop is grown. Each autumn when the chile is harvested, the smell of the roasting peppers fills the air. Each Labour Day weekend, you can gather in Hatch for the annual festival celebrating New Mexico’s famous chile.


WHEN

Annually, Labour Day weekend.

MORE INFO

Visit Hatch Chile Festival website


Santa Fe Indian Market


The Santa Fe Indian Market takes place in late August drawing crowds from around the world. Organised by the Southwestern Association for Indian Arts, this festival showcases art, pottery, jewellery and textiles by Native American artists. It has been deemed one of the greatest art fairs in the world.


WHEN

Annually, late August

Visit Santa Fe Indian Market website
